Quick Reference
Trader's Glossary

Key terms every RISEx trader should know at a glance.

Perpetual Future
A derivative with no expiry. Hold long or short indefinitely, subject to funding rates keeping it anchored to spot.
Funding Rate
Periodic payment between longs and shorts. Positive = longs pay shorts. Negative = shorts pay longs.
Mark Price
Fair-value price used to calculate unrealized PnL and trigger liquidations ‚Äî not the last traded price.
Leverage
Multiplier on your collateral. 10x leverage = 1% asset move = 10% position move. Amplifies gains and losses equally.
Liquidation
Forced position closure when collateral falls below maintenance margin. On RISEx, the engine is fully transparent and onchain.
Portfolio Margin
Margin mode where your whole portfolio backs all positions. More capital efficient ‚Äî but losses anywhere affect everything.
AutoYield
RISEx feature that routes idle collateral into yield strategies automatically while you hold open positions.
Sub-Account
Programmable child account for isolating strategies, running automated bots, or building custom trading primitives.
Composability
RISEx's ability to interact with lending, AMMs, and vaults atomically in one transaction ‚Äî unique to onchain execution.
Open Interest (OI)
Total value of all open long and short positions in a market. High OI = more market activity and potential volatility.
Index Price
Aggregate price of an asset across major spot exchanges. Mark price = index price + basis adjustment from funding.
Delta-Neutral
A position with zero net directional exposure. Used by advanced traders to earn funding rates without price risk.
